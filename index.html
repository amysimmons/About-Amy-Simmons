About me in 150 words

Hi, I'm Amy. I've been an online journalist and producer since 2009. I started my career with ABC News Online in Brisbane, writing news and feature stories and producing the home page. Since moving to Sydney in 2012 I've been a digital producer with ABC News 24 and ABC Fact Check.

My interest in coding started in 2013, when a friend recommended I attend a Rails Girls event. I couldn't believe what I'd been missing out on and knew I had to learn more! I signed up for General Assembly's 10-week Front-End Web Development course in January 2014, and loved every minute of it. I've continued learning in my spare time, but am now ready to take the next step.

I'm hoping that completing GA's Web Development Immersive course will give me the skills and confidence to work as a full-stack web developer in 2015 and for years to come.

Recent projects

I produced the Promise Tracker for ABC Fact Check throughout 2013 and 2014. The project was built by Small Multiples, a Sydney-based team of data visualisation specialists.

I built Faith in Numbers during my 10-week Front-End Web Development course with General Assembly in 2014. It uses D3.js charts to tell the story of religion in Australia.
